# Appeals System - User Guide

## For Petition Creators

### How to Submit an Appeal

1. **Navigate to Your Petition**
   - Go to your dashboard
   - Find the paused or rejected petition

2. **Click "Contact Moderator"**
   - Button appears on paused/rejected petitions
   - Opens appeal submission modal

3. **Write Your Appeal**
   - Explain why the petition should be reviewed
   - Provide any additional context or corrections
   - Be clear and respectful

4. **Submit**
   - Click "Send Message"
   - You'll receive a confirmation
   - Appeal appears in your dashboard

2. **Appeal Details**
   - Click any appeal to view full conversation
   - See all messages exchanged
   - View status history

### Replying to Moderators

1. **Open Appeal Thread**
   - Click on appeal from dashboard
   - Scroll to bottom of conversation

2. **Write Reply**
   - Type your message in the reply box
   - Provide requested information
   - Click "Send Reply"

3. **Email Notifications**
   - You'll receive emails when moderators reply
   - Click link in email to view full conversation

### Appeal Status Meanings

- **Pending**: Your appeal is in the queue, waiting for review
- **In Progress**: A moderator is actively reviewing your case
- **Resolved**: Your issue has been addressed (petition may be unpaused/approved)
- **Rejected**: Appeal was reviewed but denied (reason provided)

## For Moderators/Admins

### Accessing Appeals Dashboard

1. **Navigate to Admin Panel**
   - Click "Admin" in navigation
   - Select "Appeals" from admin menu

2. **Dashboard Overview**
   - See all appeals across all petitions
   - Filter by status
   - Search by petition title or creator name

1. **Filter Appeals**

2. **Open Appeal**

### Best Practices

#### Response Time

- Aim to respond within 24-48 hours
- Pending appeals should be reviewed first
- Set status to "In Progress" when you start reviewing

#### Communication

- Be professional and respectful
- Explain decisions clearly
- Provide specific reasons for rejections
- Offer guidance for resubmission if applicable

#### Internal Notes

- Use for complex cases requiring multiple moderators
- Document decision-making process
- Note any external factors or context

#### Status Management

- Update status as you progress
- Don't leave appeals in "Pending" too long
- Resolve or reject decisively
- Only reopen if new information emerges

### Common Scenarios

#### Scenario 1: Simple Fix Needed

1. Review appeal and petition
2. Reply with specific changes needed
3. Wait for creator to respond
4. If fixed, resolve appeal and approve petition
5. If not fixed, provide more guidance or reject

#### Scenario 2: Policy Violation

1. Review appeal and petition
2. Explain policy violation clearly
3. Reject appeal with detailed reason
4. Suggest alternative approaches if possible

#### Scenario 3: Misunderstanding

1. Review appeal and petition
2. Clarify the moderation decision
3. If petition is actually fine, resolve and approve
4. If not, explain what needs to change

#### Scenario 4: Complex Case

1. Add internal note with your thoughts
2. Consult with other moderators
3. Make decision based on consensus
4. Document reasoning in resolution notes

### Email Notifications

Moderators receive emails when:

- Moderator replies to their appeal
- Appeal status changes

**Note:** All emails include links to view full conversation in the app.

## FAQ

### For Creators

**Q: How long does it take to get a response?**
A: Typically 24-48 hours. Complex cases may take longer.

**Q: Can I submit multiple appeals for the same petition?**
A: No, only one open appeal per petition at a time.

**Q: What if my appeal is rejected?**
A: Review the rejection reason carefully. You may be able to resubmit the petition with corrections.

**Q: Can I delete an appeal?**
A: No, appeals are permanent for audit purposes.

### For Moderators

**Q: Can I assign appeals to specific moderators?**
A: Not currently. Appeals are handled on a first-come, first-served basis.

**Q: What if I need more information from the creator?**
A: Reply to the appeal asking for specific information. The creator will be notified.

**Q: Can creators see internal notes?**
A: No, internal notes are only visible to moderators and admins.

**Q: How do I handle spam appeals?**
A: Reject with reason "Spam or invalid appeal" and consider flagging the user account.
